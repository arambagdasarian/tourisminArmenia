Armenia Tourism Data Story

From Pandemic Recovery to Market Diversification (2019-2024)

The Story in Numbers

Armenia's tourism sector tells a remarkable tale of resilience. From the depths of the 2020 pandemic, when tourist arrivals plummeted to just 369,000, Armenia has not only recovered but exceeded pre-pandemic levels, reaching a peak of 2.32 million visitors in 2023—a staggering 39% year-over-year growth and 22% above 2019 levels.

However, 2024 tells a story of stabilization. Tourist arrivals dipped slightly to approximately 2.21 million, representing a 4.7% year-over-year decline. Yet this "plateau" represents a maturing market that has successfully maintained levels well above pre-pandemic benchmarks.

The Russian Factor: Concentration and Opportunity

Armenia's tourism success is inextricably linked to one dominant market: Russia. In 2023, Russian visitors accounted for approximately 49-50% of all tourist arrivals—roughly 1.14 million visitors out of 2.32 million total.

Market Concentration Analysis

While this concentration has driven rapid growth, it also creates significant risk exposure to Russian economic and political conditions.

Georgia follows as the second-largest source market at 11-12% (approximately 255,000 visitors), while Iran represents 6-8% (roughly 140,000 visitors).
